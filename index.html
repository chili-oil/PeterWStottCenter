Peter W. Stott Center

More information on the Peter W. Stott Center


The Peter W. Stott Center is more commonly known to Portland State University students as the Viking Pavillion. The building is located in the park blocks and is a staple building for athletes and coaches. The building is home to many basketball games that take place in the main court. The building includes a new weight training facility, locker rooms, an academic center, offices, a café and several student lounges, study spaces, and collaborative spaces for OHSU’s Sports Medicine Center.

Building Names Through the History

  • Physical Education (PE) during original construction, 1964-1966
  • Health & Physical Education (HPE), ca. 1966-1997
  • Peter W. Stott Center (PSC), 1997-2018
  • Peter W. Stott Center and Viking Pavilion (PSC), 2018-present

Building History

TThe building was finalized on April 5, 2018, after a 52 million dollar renovation project. It Allowed for the new facilities to be put in and expanded the arena to accommodate 3,000 seats. The building is very eco-friendly as it includes LEED Gold features that include water and energy-efficient designs. There is the use of low-emitting, non-toxic materials that were used for the building of the Pavillion, including reclaimed and stewardship council certified wood throughout, and food waste and composting facilities.
